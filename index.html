ASU Business Process Map Generator

Create a clean ISO-style business process map using numbered process steps, decision points, branches, and continuation symbols.

How to Enter Your Process

Regular process steps

  • Enter one process step per line
  • Begin every line with a unique step number followed by a period
  • Use an action verb such as Receive, Review, Enter, Submit, Approve, or Archive
  • Periods at the end of steps are removed automatically
1. Receive the agreement request 2. Enter the request into the tracking system 3. Assign the request to a team member

Decision steps

Begin a decision with [Decision], then identify the first step in each branch and the step where the branches reconnect.

2. [Decision] Is the request complete? | Yes: 3 | No: 5 | Merge: 7

In that example:

  • The Yes branch contains Steps 3–4
  • The No branch contains Steps 5–6
  • Both branches reconnect at Step 7
  • Yes, No, and Merge must point to later steps
  • Branch steps must be grouped together before the merge step
  • Decision branches may contain multiple regular process steps
  • You may use multiple decision diamonds throughout the process
